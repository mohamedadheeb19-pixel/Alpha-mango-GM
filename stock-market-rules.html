Trading Rules by G.M. Zakir Sait

A set of precise, technical rules for disciplined market entry — built on price action, RSI, and candle structure.

01
🚫

Never Buy in a Down or Flat Market

Avoid entering a trade when the market is falling or moving sideways. Buying in weakness means fighting the trend — wait for clear directional strength before committing.

02
🟢

Buy Only When the Market is Up

Enter trades only when the market is in an upward move. Buying in the direction of momentum increases the probability of a successful trade significantly.

03
📉

RSI Must Cross Above the SMA

The RSI line should cross above its Simple Moving Average (SMA) before entry. This crossover signals a shift in momentum from weak to strong — a key confirmation trigger.

04
🔺

RSI Must Break Above Previous High

The RSI should also exceed its most recent swing high. This confirms that bullish momentum is accelerating, not just recovering — a stronger signal for a high-quality entry.

05
🦶

Right Foot Rule — Max 12 Candles

The most recent bullish move (before the current pullback) must have occurred within the last 12 candles. If the market's last "up leg" is more than 12 candles behind, the setup is too old and the momentum has likely faded. Only trade when the bullish structure is fresh and recent.

The Basics

Core Fundamentals Every Investor Must Know

Before you invest a single rupee or dollar, understand these foundational concepts that govern every profitable decision.

01
📈

Understand What You Buy

Never invest in a company you don't understand. Study the business model, revenue sources, and competitive advantages before committing capital.

02
⏳

Time in Market Beats Timing

Staying invested consistently over years outperforms trying to predict short-term market movements. Compounding rewards patience.

03
🎯

Set Clear Goals

Define your financial goals — retirement, education, wealth creation — and align your investment strategy to your timeline and risk appetite.

04
🛡️

Capital Preservation First

Protecting your principal is more important than chasing returns. Losing 50% requires a 100% gain just to break even. Never risk more than you can afford to lose.

05
📊

Read Financial Statements

Learn to read balance sheets, P&L statements, and cash flow reports. Numbers tell the real story of a company's health.

06
🌐

Diversify Across Sectors

Don't concentrate in one sector. Spread investments across industries so a downturn in one doesn't wipe out your entire portfolio.

Elite Principles

The Golden Rules of Profitable Investing

These timeless rules have guided the world's greatest investors — from Warren Buffett to Rakesh Jhunjhunwala.

💰

Buy Low, Sell High — But Know What "Low" Means

Don't buy stocks that are cheap for bad reasons. Focus on companies trading below their intrinsic value due to temporary market fear, not structural problems.

Tip: Use P/E ratio, P/B ratio, and DCF analysis to estimate intrinsic value.
🔄

Reinvest Your Dividends

Dividends reinvested compound your wealth exponentially over time. This is one of the most underrated strategies for long-term wealth building.

Tip: In India, DRIP (Dividend Reinvestment Plans) can amplify CAGR significantly.
✂️

Cut Losses, Let Winners Run

Set a stop-loss discipline. Exit losing trades before small losses become catastrophic. Conversely, don't book profits too early from fundamentally strong stocks.

Tip: Many successful traders use a 7–8% stop-loss rule as a standard practice.
🧠

Control Your Emotions — Fear and Greed are Enemies

Markets are driven by emotion in the short term. The investor who can stay rational during crashes and euphoria gains a massive edge over the crowd.

Tip: Write down your investment thesis before buying. Review it when panic sets in.
📅

Use Systematic Investment (SIP) for Consistency

Investing a fixed amount regularly — regardless of market conditions — averages your cost over time and removes the burden of market timing entirely.

Tip: Even ₹5,000/month in an index fund over 20 years creates significant wealth via SIP.

Approach

Three Proven Profit Strategies

Pick a strategy that matches your temperament, time availability, and risk tolerance.

📦 Value Investing

  • Buy undervalued quality stocks
  • Hold for 3–10+ years
  • Focus on fundamentals
  • Ignore short-term noise
  • Reinvest dividends
  • Low transaction frequency

🚀 Growth Investing

  • Buy high-growth potential companies
  • Focus on revenue growth rate
  • Accept higher valuations (P/E)
  • Target emerging sectors
  • Technology, pharma, EV sectors
  • Medium to long term horizon

📉 Index Investing

  • Buy Nifty/Sensex index funds
  • Lowest cost strategy
  • Market-matching returns
  • No stock-picking needed
  • Ideal for busy investors
  • Set-and-forget approach

📊 Momentum Trading

  • Trade short-term price trends
  • Use technical analysis
  • Strict stop-losses required
  • High discipline needed
  • Focus on volume & RSI signals
  • Not suitable for beginners
Warning Signs

Mistakes That Kill Profits

Avoid these common errors that cause most retail investors to lose money in the market.

✕

Investing on Tips & Rumours

Hot tips from social media, WhatsApp groups, or friends rarely hold up. Always do your own research before investing.

✕

Overtrading and Excessive Churning

Frequent buying and selling racks up brokerage fees, STT, and taxes — eating away at your returns even when you're "winning."

✕

Investing Emergency Money

Never invest money you might need within 1–2 years. Markets can stay down for extended periods and force you to sell at losses.

✕

Ignoring Taxes & Costs

Short-term capital gains tax (STCG), long-term capital gains tax (LTCG), STT, and brokerage fees can significantly reduce actual returns.

✕

Panic Selling During Corrections

Markets correct regularly — 10–20% drops are normal. Selling in panic locks in losses and keeps you out when recovery happens.

✕

Using Leverage You Don't Understand

Margin trading and F&O (Futures & Options) can multiply both gains AND losses. They've wiped out many unprepared retail traders.

The Legends

Rules from the World's Greatest Investors

These individuals built billions and helped millions grow their wealth. Their rules are battle-tested across decades of markets.

🧓

Warren Buffett

Chairman of Berkshire Hathaway · Net Worth ~$130B · 60+ years of market-beating returns
📏

Rule #1: Never Lose Money

Rule #2: Never forget Rule #1. Capital preservation is the foundation of all wealth building. Avoid permanent loss at all costs.

🏰

Buy Companies with Wide Moats

Invest in businesses with durable competitive advantages — strong brands, low costs, network effects — that protect profits for decades.

😴

Be Greedy When Others Are Fearful

The best buying opportunities come during market panics and crashes. When everyone is selling out of fear, the best prices appear.

🪨

Only Buy What You'd Hold Forever

If you wouldn't be comfortable holding a stock for 10 years, don't hold it for 10 minutes. Long-term conviction beats short-term speculation.

📘

Peter Lynch

Manager of Fidelity Magellan Fund · Averaged 29.2% annual returns over 13 years
👁️

Invest in What You Know

Everyday consumers often spot great investments before Wall Street does. Use your own experience and observations as a research advantage.

🔍

Do Your Homework

Never invest based on a tip alone. Understand the company's story, earnings growth, and why it will be worth more in the future.

💎

Find "Ten-Baggers" Early

Look for small, undiscovered companies with strong growth potential. A stock that grows 10x is a ten-bagger — Lynch made his fortune finding them early.

🧩

Ignore Short-Term Market Noise

The stock market is full of daily noise that doesn't matter. Focus on the underlying business — if it keeps growing, the stock will follow eventually.

📚

Benjamin Graham

Father of Value Investing · Author of "The Intelligent Investor" · Mentor to Warren Buffett
🛡️

Always Demand a Margin of Safety

Only buy a stock when it trades significantly below its intrinsic value. This cushion protects you from errors in analysis and unexpected downturns.

🤖

Mr. Market is Your Servant, Not Advisor

The market offers prices daily — sometimes rational, often emotional. Use its mood swings to your advantage. Never let it dictate your decisions.

📐

Separate Investment from Speculation

An investment promises safety of principal and adequate return. Anything else is speculation. Know which one you're doing at all times.

🏗️

Focus on Net Asset Value

Buy companies trading below their net asset value (book value). This deep value approach protects against downside and sets up strong gains.

🇮🇳

Rakesh Jhunjhunwala

India's Big Bull · Turned ₹5,000 into ₹40,000+ crore · Icon of Indian stock markets
🇮🇳

Bet on India's Long-Term Growth

Believe in the macro story of your country. Jhunjhunwala's biggest wins came from betting on India's economic growth decades before it was obvious.

💪

Have Conviction and Hold Through Volatility

He held Titan Company for over 20 years through multiple downturns. True wealth comes from conviction-based holding, not panic selling.

🌱

Identify Sectors Before They Boom

Spot emerging industries early — retail, aviation, financials — before they become mainstream. Early entry multiplies returns dramatically.

❤️

Love the Market — Respect Its Power

Never fight the market's direction. Respect its power while staying patient. The market rewards those who understand and work with it, not against it.

♟️

George Soros

Founder of Quantum Fund · "The Man Who Broke the Bank of England" · $1B profit in a single day
🌍

Markets Are Always Biased

Markets never perfectly reflect reality — they are always influenced by participant bias. Find where the bias exists and position accordingly before it corrects.

📊

It's Not Whether You're Right or Wrong

What matters is how much you make when right and how little you lose when wrong. Asymmetric risk-reward is the key to big profits.

🔄

Reflexivity — Prices Affect Fundamentals

Rising stock prices can improve a company's fundamentals. Understand this feedback loop to anticipate market moves before they happen.

⚡

When You See It — Act Big

When a rare, high-conviction opportunity appears, commit fully. Soros bet $10B against the British pound because he was certain. Conviction must match position size.

🧠

Charlie Munger

Vice Chairman of Berkshire Hathaway · Buffett's partner · Pioneer of mental model investing
🧱

Invert, Always Invert

Instead of asking "how do I succeed?" ask "what would make me fail?" Avoid stupidity consistently and success follows. Thinking backwards reveals hidden risks.

🗂️

Build a Latticework of Mental Models

Draw from psychology, economics, engineering, and history to make better decisions. No single discipline gives the full picture of a business or market.

⏳

Patience is the Ultimate Edge

Sit on your hands and wait for the perfect pitch. You don't have to swing at every opportunity — in investing, doing nothing is often the best action.

🌟

Buy Great Companies at Fair Prices

A wonderful company at a fair price beats a fair company at a wonderful price every time. Quality of the business matters more than cheapness of the stock.

Do I understand the company's core business model?
✓
Have I reviewed the last 3 years of financial statements?
✓
Is the stock trading below or near its intrinsic value?
✓
Is the company debt level manageable and sustainable?
✓
What is my exit plan — both target price and stop-loss?
✓
Am I investing money I don't need for at least 3 years?
✓
Is this stock fitting within my overall portfolio allocation?
✓
Have I checked industry trends and sector outlook?
✓
Am I investing based on research, not emotion or FOMO?
